Anne Stimson.

A professional illustrator.

Welcome to my world of creativity and imagination! I'm thrilled to have you here as I share my journey from a young bookworm with a tattered copy of 'Pinocchio' to a seasoned illustrator, graphic designer, and aspiring writer.

As a child, I found solace and inspiration within the pages of classic tales like 'The Famous Five' by Enid Blyton, 'The Secret Garden' by Frances Hodgson Burnett, and that cherished, well-worn 'Pinocchio' by Birn Publishers. That ancient copy of 'Pinocchio,' held together by tape and fraying at the edges, held a special place in my heart. Its vivid illustrations breathed life into the characters, making them feel like dear friends.

After leaving school, I yearned to attend art school. However, my passion for art led me to become a teaching assistant at a primary school for a year, as I had to wait until I was old enough to pursue my dreams. Finally, I embarked on my artistic journey at Camberwell School of Art in London, where I obtained my degree in Graphic Design with a specialisation in illustration. Following that, I completed a year of teacher training, and from there, my career began to take shape.

During my student years, I was profoundly influenced by artists like Carl Larsson, Arthur Rackham, Aubrey Beardsley, and Maurice Sendak. Today, my favourite contemporary author/illustrators are Oliver Jeffers and Anna Kemp. Their work continues to inspire and drive my passion for storytelling and illustration.

I am proud to have received the Times Educational Award for my work on 'Tournaments,' a captivating book by Richard Barber on medieval tournaments. My creative journey has also led me to illustrate for magazines, design posters for Cambridge University Press, and contribute a full-page illustration to 'Cash,' a prestigious German Financial Newspaper. My art has graced the walls of galleries such as the David Paul Gallery in Chichester, The Cork Gallery in London, and the esteemed Chris Beetles Gallery in St James, London.

Beyond my artistic pursuits, I'm passionate about playing bridge. My husband and I run a vibrant bridge club in Wandsworth, London, UK, where I call home. I discovered bridge during a challenging period of illness, and it proved to be a remarkable aid in my recovery. Like the stroke of a brush on canvas, bridge allowed me to regain my sense of purpose and connection.

I'm honoured to be a member of the Society of Authors and Illustrators, where I continue to learn, grow, and connect with fellow creatives who share my passion for storytelling and art. I invite you to join me on this exciting creative journey as I explore the world of fiction and dive deeper into writing and illustrating picture books. Together, let's celebrate the magic of stories, the beauty of art, and the resilience of the human spirit.
