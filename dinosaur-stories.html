Dinosaur Jim Goes Sailing

Up on the roof Dinosaur Jim and Ollie the cat

Were practicing their magic act

‘It’s too hot for magic,’ Rosie Bee said

‘Let’s go to the park instead.’

Up the busy street they ran

Past Mr Croc and his van

Past red London buses, taxis and scooters

Shops selling postcards, socks and computers.

‘Here’s the park,’ cried Dinosaur Jim

‘Let’s hire a boat’ said Ollie

‘Look, the boatman’s standing there

And we’ve just enough money for the fare.’

‘I’ll row,’ said Jim as Ollie climbed in

And the bees settled down on the cushions.

Jim dipped his oars in the cool blue water

And Ollie tried some fishing…….when

VROOOM VROOOM! ‘Watch out!’ It was Mr Croc.

Driving his speedboat and messing about

‘You’re in my way,’ yelled Mr Croc

And then Jim’s boat began to rock.

Mr Croc laughed as he hurtled by

Splashing Ollie in the eye

Then Jim gave a shout

As their boat tipped over and they all fell out.

Jim lifted Ollie onto his head

‘The water isn’t deep,’ he said

And then he yelled ‘Ollie! I’m stuck.

My feet won’t move in this sludgy muck.’

‘Hang on Jim,’ buzzed Rosie Bee

‘There’s a rope over there that I can see.’

‘Oh please hurry up,’ yelled Dinosaur Jim

‘I’ve got a big SLIMY FROG on my chin.’

The bees untied the rope from the tree

And Ollie cried ‘We’ll soon be free!’

‘Now PULL,’ buzzed Rosie ‘And mind you don’t fall.’

But Ollie and Jim didn’t move at all.

Then Ollie shouted ‘Look up there?

What’s that floating in the air?

It’s not a kite or cloud or bird

NO! The bees have turned into a word.’

Then on the shore they saw a lady with a pram

A boy on a bike and an ice-cream van

A jogger with a dog, a fireman with a ladder,

A man with a stick and a tourist with a camera.

There was a policeman dressed in blue,

An elephant from the zoo,

A bouncy kangaroo, a duck and a goose

And a strange looking moose.

‘Now listen everyone,’ buzzed Rosie Bee

‘Pull as hard as you can on that rope by the tree.’

And everyone pulled but it wasn’t any good

Ollie and Jim stayed exactly where they stood.

Then a little brown mouse ran down the slope

And curled his tail round the end of the rope.

‘Can I help?’ he squeaked and everyone laughed

‘If only’, they said ‘you were a rhino or giraffe.’

‘You see little mouse you’re no good us

‘What we need,’ quacked the duck ‘is a great big bus.’

‘YES!’ Boomed the elephant ‘Without a doubt

A great big bus would pull Jim and Ollie out.’

‘BUT!’ Boomed the elephant ‘There’s no bus about,

So let’s pull together with the little brown mouse.’

‘Ready?’ cried the policeman dressed in blue

‘Pull!’ cried the goose and the bouncy kangaroo

‘HURRAH!’ Cried the man in the ice cream van

When Ollie and Jim were pulled to dry land.

Then from the pond they heard a shout

And there was Mr Croc prancing about

‘Hey!’ He yelled ‘I’m right out of diesel and I’ve only one oar!

Just get me the boatman to tow me to shore.’

‘HA!’ shouted Ollie ‘You’ll just have to wait’

‘The boatman,’ cried Jim ‘Is on his break,’

‘YES,’ buzzed Rosie and all the Bees

He’s sitting in the cafe having his tea.’

‘So ice-cream all around?’ said the lady with the pram

‘Right,’ said the man in the ice cream van

‘Strawberry ones for us,’ cried Ollie and Jim

And an extra large one for the little brown mouse.’

So even if you’re little and the smallest in your house

You can still make a difference like the little brown mouse.
